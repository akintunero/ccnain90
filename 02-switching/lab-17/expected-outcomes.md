## Expected Outcomes – Lab 11 Switching and Redundancy

Upon completing this lab, learners should be able to:

1. **Design a small campus Layer 2 topology**
   - Place access and distribution switches logically.
   - Allocate VLANs to user, voice, server, guest, and management domains.
2. **Implement VLANs and trunks across multiple switches**
   - Create VLANs consistently on all switches.
   - Configure 802.1Q trunk links with appropriate allowed VLAN lists.
3. **Deploy basic spanning-tree-based redundancy**
   - Elect and verify a deliberate STP root bridge.
   - Interpret STP port roles and states on access and distribution switches.
4. **Maintain inter-VLAN connectivity during link failures**
   - Validate that traffic reconverges when an uplink goes down.
   - Observe the differences in behaviour between blocked and forwarding redundant links.
5. **Document a resilient access-layer design**
   - Produce topology and addressing documentation that matches the implemented configuration.

These outcomes provide the switching and redundancy foundation required for subsequent routing and WAN labs.
